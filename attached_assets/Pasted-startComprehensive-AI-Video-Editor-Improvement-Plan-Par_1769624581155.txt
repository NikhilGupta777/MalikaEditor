startComprehensive AI Video Editor Improvement Plan
Part 1: Current AI Limitations & What's Preventing True Autonomy
🔴 Problem: AI Can't Explain Its Reasoning

Current State: The AI makes decisions but doesn't record WHY. When it chooses video over image, or decides on a specific cut, there's no persisted reasoning.

Impact:

    Users can't understand AI decisions
    Learning system can't learn from reasoning patterns
    No way to improve decision quality over time

Solution: Create a DecisionReasoning schema that captures:

{
  decision: "selected video over image",
  factors: [
    { factor: "high motion detected", weight: 0.8, source: "motionAnalysis" },
    { factor: "action sequence overlap", weight: 0.6, source: "actionSequences" }
  ],
  confidence: 85,
  alternatives: ["stock_image_3", "ai_image_1"],
  whyNotAlternatives: ["static content doesn't match dynamic scene"]
}

🔴 Problem: Hardcoded Rules Override AI Intelligence

Currently Hardcoded:

    MOTION_KEYWORDS array - AI uses keyword matching instead of actual motion analysis
    B-roll duration rules: fast=2-3s, moderate=3-4s, slow=4-6s (fixed)
    validateAndFixBrollActions imposes 3-second minimum spacing, 2-10 second duration limits
    Caption styling (font, colors, margins) is completely fixed
    MAX_VISION_CANDIDATES = 30 limits how many options AI can consider

Solution: Replace hardcoded rules with AI-driven decisions:

    Let AI determine optimal B-roll duration based on actual content, not just pacing category
    Allow AI to suggest caption styling based on video tone/genre
    Remove artificial limits on what AI can consider

What This Data SHOULD Do:

    Speakers → Smart Cuts: Don't cut in the middle of someone speaking; prefer cuts during speaker transitions
    Speakers → B-roll Placement: Add B-roll when the same speaker talks for too long (visual variety)
    EmotionFlow → Pacing: Speed up during low-emotion segments, slow down for emotional peaks
    EmotionFlow → Music/Audio: AI could suggest music mood matching emotional arc
    Entities → Smart B-roll: If speaker mentions "Eiffel Tower", search for that exact entity
    Sentiment → Caption Styling: Positive = brighter colors, negative = more subdued
    Chapters → Auto-structure: Use detected chapters as natural segment boundaries
    Scenes → Transition Points: Natural transitions at scene boundaries, not arbitrary timestamps
    Silent Segments → Auto-cut Candidates: Automatically suggest cutting extended silences
